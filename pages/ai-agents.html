AI Agents in Transcriptomics

AI agents represent the frontier of transcriptomics analysis, integrating large language models with specialized computational pipelines to automate complex, multi-step analytical workflows. These systems can understand natural language instructions from users, decompose analysis tasks into actionable steps, execute specialized tools, and iterate based on feedback—without requiring users to manually chain individual methods. Our review identifies 5 emerging AI agent systems designed for transcriptomics applications.

AI Agent Capabilities & Accessibility

Total AI Agents: 5

Online Accessible: 2 (CellAgent, CompBioAgent)

Note: SpatialAgent source code is not publicly available

Key Insights: AI agents in transcriptomics exhibit diverse deployment models. While most require either offline GPU resources or online server access, the trend is toward cloud-based solutions that democratize AI-driven analysis. The mixture of open-source (AutoBA, STAgent) and commercial/restricted systems suggests both community-driven development and proprietary research initiatives. These agents typically integrate multiple task-specific tools, foundation models, and LLMs to provide end-to-end analysis workflows from raw data interpretation to biological insight generation.
